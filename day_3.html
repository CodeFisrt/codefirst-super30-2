Sachin Tendulkar

Biography

Sachin Tendulkar, born April 24, 1973, in Mumbai, is an Indian cricket legend known as the "Master Blaster," famous for holding numerous batting records, including the most international runs and 100 international centuries (51 Tests, 49 ODIs), leading India to the 2011 World Cup, and being the only sportsperson awarded India's highest civilian honour, the Bharat Ratna. Debuting at 16 in 1989, his career spanned over two decades, defined by discipline, iconic strokes like the uppercut, and overcoming injuries, retiring in 2013 as a global icon who inspired millions and earned accolades like the Padma Vibhushan.

Career Highlights & Records

  • Master of Batting: First player to score an ODI double century (200*) and holds records for most runs in Tests & ODIs.
  • Centuries: The only player with 100 international centuries (51 Tests, 49 ODIs).
  • World Cup Glory: Key player in India's 2011 World Cup victory.

Key Milestones & Awards

  • Awards: Received Arjuna Award (1994), Khel Ratna (1997), Padma Shri (1998), Padma Vibhushan (2008).
  • Bharat Ratna: Awarded in 2013, becoming the first sportsperson to receive it.
  • Retirement: Played his final Test in 2013 at Wankhede Stadium.
